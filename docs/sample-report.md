## 1. Auto-Renewal / Evergreen - Risk: MEDIUM

**Rationale:** Three-year initial term that auto-renews for one-year periods
unless either party gives 90 days' written notice. Notice window is reasonable
but renewal pricing is at vendor's "sole discretion" up to 7%.

> "This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year
> renewal terms (each, a 'Renewal Term'), unless either party provides
> written notice of non-renewal not less than ninety (90) days prior to
> the end of the then-current term. Fees for any Renewal Term may be
> increased by Vendor in its sole discretion, provided such increase
> shall not exceed seven percent (7%) of the prior term's fees."

**Case-law:**

- *ProCD, Inc. v. Zeidenberg*, 86 F.3d 1447 (7th Cir. 1996) -
  Standardized renewal/assent terms enforceable absent unconscionability.
- *Schnabel v. Trilegiant Corp.*, 697 F.3d 110 (2d Cir. 2012) -
  Auto-renewal terms unenforceable where notice was inconspicuous.
- *Williams v. Walker-Thomas Furniture Co.*, 350 F.2d 445 (D.C. Cir. 1965) -
  Unconscionable adhesion terms (incl. renewal) may be voided.

## 2. Indemnification / Hold-Harmless - Risk: HIGH

**Rationale:** One-way indemnity from Customer to Vendor, *including* claims
arising from Vendor's own negligence, with no cap.

> "Customer shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Vendor and its
> officers, directors, employees, agents, affiliates, successors, and
> assigns from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, liabilities,
> costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out
> of or relating to ... irrespective of any negligence on the part of
> Vendor."

**Case-law:**

- *Hooper Assocs., Ltd. v. AGS Computers, Inc.*, 74 N.Y.2d 487 (1989) -
  Indemnity for first-party claims requires unmistakably clear language.
- *Ethyl Corp. v. Daniel Constr. Co.*, 725 S.W.2d 705 (Tex. 1987) -
  Express-negligence rule: indemnity for own negligence must be express.

## 3. Limitation of Liability / Damages Cap - Risk: MEDIUM

**Rationale:** Cap is 3 months of fees - aggressive, may not survive a
material breach where damages dwarf the cap. Standard consequential damages
waiver is mutual.

> "IN NO EVENT SHALL VENDOR'S AGGREGATE LIABILITY ARISING OUT OF OR
> RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT EXCEED THE AMOUNTS PAID BY CUSTOMER TO VENDOR
> UNDER THIS AGREEMENT IN THE THREE (3) MONTHS PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING
> RISE TO LIABILITY."

**Case-law:**

- *Hadley v. Baxendale*, 9 Ex. 341 (1854) - Foreseeability rule for
  consequential damages.
- *Metro. Life Ins. Co. v. Noble Lowndes Int'l, Inc.*, 84 N.Y.2d 430 (1994) -
  Caps enforceable absent gross negligence / willful misconduct.
- *Kalisch-Jarcho, Inc. v. City of New York*, 58 N.Y.2d 377 (1983) -
  Exculpatory clauses unenforceable for intentional or grossly negligent acts.

## 4. Governing Law / Forum Selection - Risk: LOW

**Rationale:** Delaware law + Delaware courts is a defensible, neutral choice
for a Delaware-incorporated vendor; well-developed commercial case law.

> "This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with
> the laws of the State of Delaware ... The parties irrevocably submit
> to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located
> in New Castle County, Delaware."

**Case-law:**

- *M/S Bremen v. Zapata Off-Shore Co.*, 407 U.S. 1 (1972) - Forum-selection
  clauses are prima facie valid.
- *Atl. Marine Constr. Co. v. U.S. Dist. Court*, 571 U.S. 49 (2013) - Valid
  forum clauses enforced under § 1404(a).

## 5. Change of Control / Assignment - Risk: MEDIUM

**Rationale:** Deemed-assignment trigger at >50% voting securities can be
weaponized in M&A; consent requirement is qualified ("not unreasonably
withheld"), which mitigates somewhat.

> "any merger, consolidation, sale of all or substantially all of a
> party's assets, or change of control involving more than fifty percent
> (50%) of the voting securities of a party shall be deemed an assignment
> of this Agreement requiring the other party's prior written consent."

**Case-law:**

- *SQL Solutions, Inc. v. Oracle Corp.*, 1991 WL 626458 (N.D. Cal. 1991) -
  Reverse triangular merger treated as assignment.
- *Meso Scale Diagnostics v. Roche*, 62 A.3d 62 (Del. Ch. 2013) - Whether
  merger = assignment depends on the contractual language.
- *Cincom Sys., Inc. v. Novelis Corp.*, 581 F.3d 431 (6th Cir. 2009) -
  Statutory merger transferred software license; no consent obtained = breach.

Recommended negotiation priorities (highest -> lowest):

1. **Indemnification** - cap it, mutualize it, and exclude Vendor's own
   negligence/willful misconduct.
2. **Liability cap** - bump from 3 months of fees to 12 months; carve out
   data-loss, IP infringement, and confidentiality.
3. **Change of control** - either drop the deemed-assignment trigger or
   carve out internal reorganizations and good-faith successor entities.
4. **Auto-renewal** - extend notice window and cap renewal price increases
   at CPI.
5. **Jurisdiction** - acceptable as drafted.
